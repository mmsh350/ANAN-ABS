About Us

Our Story & Values

ANAN University Business School (ABS) is an innovative effort of the ANAN University Kwall, Nigeria, established as a non profit private university with a mission to provide professionals and researchers the modernity driving leading educational institutions in this 21st century world. The university is licensed by the National Universities Commission in Nigeria and is the making of the Association of National Accoutnants of Nigeria (ANAN) which is Africa's leading body of professional accountants established in 1979 with the unique mandate of 'advancing the science of accountancy'. With diverse membership accross the African continent ANAN is positioned to invest in promoting accountancy and the overall management field towards eloquence in research and efficient practices for a better world.

ABS is presently leading the birth of a pan-African Association of Collegiates of Business in Africa (ACBA) in order to promote precipitation of university business schools and provide avenue for regionally compatible accrediation system towards quality assurance and accessibility. Our professional courses and research capabilities are designed for global excellence. We are focused on contributing to the needs of our communities and creating a lasting impact that empowers people to achieve their goals. Our graduates' goal is to become ethical and astute thinkers who are competitive in the global economy. And their experience at ABS shapes their self- esteem and their future career.

As all modern humans can trace their ancestry back to Africa, we proud ourselves as key movers in providing educational solutions to our descendants dispersed across all continents without boundary. ABS operates from both the University's premises at Kwall in Plateau State and its liaison and online learning centre in Abuja, all in Nigeria. The School's learning environment and facilities were designed with the inquiring mind in mind - serenity that spurs thinking and facilities that encourage perseverance, making our learning culture a fun.

ABS is committed to unlocking students' potential as future leaders and change-makers. As we become an even more integral part of African and global communities, we remain focused on transforming lives through research, teaching and academic excellence. Outreach

Commitment is a defining characteristic of the university's identity and of our academic culture and disciplines. As important partners of the community,the university is characterized by the development and application of knowledge in local contexts.

  COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT

ABS builds sustainable partnerships with local organizations, residents, government, industry and businesses. This collaboration is fulfilling and strengthens both the university and the community through the transfer of progress and knowledge, enhancing the quality and vibrancy of life in Africa and world over. Our goals are achieved through various outreach programs and research projects. We also support commercial initiatives and provide access to continuing and professional education opportunities.

  WIDENING PARTICIPATION

Since its creation, the University has made a significant contribution to expanding participation in higher education in this field and this remains one of our priorities. Our increased participation strategy targets primarily socioeconomically disadvantaged groups, but also includes indigenous communities and other groups.

  BUILDING BETTER RELATIONSHIPS WITH SCHOOLS

The development of student engagement programs at ABS includes feedback from government, public and independent schools, as well as education priority areas determined at both national and regional levels

  SOCIAL INCLUSION

Inclusion and equity are core values at ABS, and diversity is one of our great strengths. ABS reflects the broader global community, including students and staff of all backgrounds, ages, races, ethnicities, genders, sexualities, religions, political beliefs, disabilities and life experiences.

  REGIONAL CHALLENGES INITIATIVE

In the coming decades, we will face many challenges and changes to our way of life. The Regional Challenges Initiative (RCI) recognizes the interconnectedness of these transformations. This program encourages and develops creative, community-focused, interdisciplinary research that helps foster social, economic and cultural change in our region and and will be replicated across the globe
